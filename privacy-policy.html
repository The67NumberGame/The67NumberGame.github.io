Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

This page explains what information is collected when you use The 67 Number Game, how it's used, and what choices you have. We've kept it as simple and plain as possible.

What we collect

You don't need to create an account or give us any personal details to play. We don't collect your name, email address, or anything like that directly.

However, like most websites, some information is collected automatically when you visit:

  • Analytics data — We use Google Analytics, which records things like which pages you visit, how long you spend on the site, what device and browser you're using, and your approximate location (country or region level, not your address). This data is anonymised.
  • Advertising cookies — We show ads through Google AdSense. Google uses cookies to serve and personalise those ads. This is handled entirely by Google and governed by their privacy policy.

What we do with it

The analytics data helps us understand how people use the site so we can improve it. The advertising cookies are there to keep the site free to use — ads are how we cover costs. We don't sell your data, and we don't share it with anyone outside of the third-party services listed below.

Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. We use them for analytics (Google Analytics) and advertising (Google AdSense). You can disable cookies in your browser settings at any time, though this may affect how the site works. You can also opt out of Google's ad personalisation at adssettings.google.com.

Third-party services we use

The following third-party services may process data when you use our site. Each has its own privacy policy:

  • Google Analytics — policies.google.com/privacy
  • Google AdSense — policies.google.com/privacy
  • Google Fonts — fonts.google.com

We don't control what these services do with data — that's between you and them.

Accessibility

The game is suitable for all ages but is not specifically directed at children under 13. We don't knowingly collect any personal information from children. If you're concerned about a child's data, get in touch and we'll sort it out.

Your data rights

If you're based in the UK or EU, you have rights under data protection law — including the right to access, correct, or request deletion of data held about you. Since we don't directly store personal data ourselves, most of these rights would be exercised through Google's own privacy tools. For anything else, contact us and we'll help where we can.

Changes to this policy

If we make significant changes to this policy, we'll update the date at the top of this page. Carrying on using the site after a change means you're okay with the update.

Questions?

If anything here is unclear, feel free to get in touch.
